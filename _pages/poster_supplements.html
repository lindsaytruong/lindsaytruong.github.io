Exploratory Data Analysis

Analysis began by segmenting the data. We chose MTF location after ranking MTF’s by number of cases. After selecting the top 5 locations with the most amount of cases, we analyzed their variation in timeliness. Ft. Hood showed the least amount of variation and was selected as the basis for our analysis.


Least-Squares Anomaly Detection (LSAD)

LSAD (figure on the right) models as a classification problem. It defines an inlier class as the mean of the process and finds the conditional probability of an observation being a member of this class. The complement is the Anomaly Score and scores close to 1.0 signals anomalous behavior.


Density-based spatial clustering of applications with noise (DBSCAN)

DBSCAN detects clusters based on an epsilon distance measure between points and a minimum cluster size to identify points outside of clusters as noise which are considered to be anomalous. This DBSCAN identifies points that fall outside a specified cluster. Although some points initially appear as anomalous (Jan. 2018 and Jan. 2019), the peaks have a recurring pattern and therefore fall within the identified cluster.

About ☕

Hello! My name is Amanda and I’m a current master’s student in the inaugural class of the University of Virginia School of Data Science. Prior to the program, I attended the University of Michigan - Ann Arbor, where I graduated with high honors in economics and a minor in mathematics, studied abroad as a Gilman Scholar in Beijing, interned for the Ministry of Economic Development in Albania, trained and competed internationally in taekwondo, and held various jobs including as a Research Assistant and Data Visualization Consultant. In my free time, I publish articles to Medium's Towards Data Science publication.

The spark in me for programming ignited the first time a teacher taught me how to use my knowledge of matrix algebra to populate and loop through a vector. It was stoked everytime I picked up a new language (R, C++, Python, SQL, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, MATLAB) and molded the code for projects in physics, UX/UI design, machine-learning, NLP, or geospatial data visualization, to name a few. Everytime I jumped from my seat, ecstatic that a null hypothesis could be rejected or my confusion matrix had a particularly low false positive rate, my immense passion for programming and for data science was stoked further.

My qualitative skills include written and oral communication, leadership experience, creative problem-solving, and team management. I’m excited to find employment (May 2021) that will help me further hone my skills with data and creative analysis. Thanks for reading about me today!
